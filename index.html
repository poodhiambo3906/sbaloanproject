SMALL BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION (SBA) LOANS

The SBA helps small businesses get loans. The SBA works with lenders to provide loans to small businesses.
The agency doesn’t lend money directly to small business owners. Instead, it sets guidelines for loans made by its partnering lenders, community development organizations, and micro-lending institutions. The SBA reduces risk for lenders and makes it easier for them to access capital. That makes it easier for small businesses to get loans.
